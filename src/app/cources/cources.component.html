The changes of the last 50 years have rendered the political and social world far too complex for most of us to understand.
I often call democracy the broadest and most complex relationship you can never have. Unlike academics and scholars whose understanding of these issues come mostly from research and the deliberation of abstract ideas, most of my insights were first triggered by excruciating first-hand experience. I have been extraordinarily privileged to be able to help people understand their own thinking processes, and to work with them when they are at their most vulnerable. This has given me a deep appreciation of what it takes for us humans to get along with each other (and ourselves). Every functional relationship, I have come to realize, is a miracle of love, patience, and compromise.
MetaCulture’s courses are committed to developing intelligent and thoughtful citizens who can help sustain our extremely vulnerable societies. A lifetime of working with groups and communities (to strengthen their collaboration and conflict management processes) has taught me that sustainable communities depend more on the culture and character of their people than on technocrats with their clever attempts at developing policies, legislation, or social engineering. These courses are designed for those who are willing to question themselves, their assumptions, and their solutions for all the ills of the world.
