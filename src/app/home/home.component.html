    Ashok Panikkar
    Over the past many years, I have consulted on complex disputes and conflicts, and managed relationships between racially, religiously,
    sexually, ethnically, and ideologically different groups. I have given innumerable talks across the world and taught people how to think differently about communication, conflict, diversity, democracy, relationships, and- themselves. Here’s what I have learned: Working in the housing sector, I learned about the reality of unequal power between tenants and landlords. Working in low-income neighborhoods, I learned about the vulnerabilities of homogeneous communities as ‘outsiders’ moved in. Working with Hindu/ Muslim conflict I discovered the realities of negotiating conflicting value systems. Being an immigrant and working with immigrants I understood how cultural differences could not just be brushed aside with platitudes about tolerance or respect. Most importantly working to create and sustain ‘flat’, ‘participatory’ and ‘collaborative’ organizations, I developed a healthy respect for anyone who tries to run a genuinely liberal and
    democratic nation. MetaCulture’s courses and artwork come out of my work of over the past forty years. They are designed to help intelligent adults think differently and deeply about our personal role in sustaining culture, society, and civilization. I survived a master’s program in Critical and Creative Thinking in the USA, and an undergraduate program in Visual Communication in India. I founded MetaCulture in 2005 in Bangalore India- and for good measure, we were once awarded the International Leadership Award by the Association for Conflict Resolution, USA in 2010.

    During the past few years, our online courses have attracted students from around the world. Much of what I know today, I owe to my students and clients who were gracious enough to invite me into their messy worlds. I live in Washington DC and when I am not teaching, creating posters and cooking, am working on my much-delayed book on the many misconceptions about democracy, pluralism, and what it means to be a civilized human.
